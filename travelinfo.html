Want to come to Botswana?

Here is important information regarding your travel and stay in Botswana.

Visa Requirements

Most visitors can obtain a tourist visa upon arrival. However, check with your local embassy or the Botswana Ministry of Immigration for specific requirements based on your nationality.

Health & Safety

It is recommended to get vaccinations for Yellow Fever, Hepatitis A & B, and Typhoid. Malaria prophylaxis is advised when traveling to certain regions like the Okavango Delta and Chobe.

Currency

The official currency is the Botswana Pula (BWP). Credit cards are widely accepted in cities, but carry cash when visiting remote areas.

Best Time to Visit

The dry season (May to October) is ideal for wildlife viewing. The wet season (November to April) is lush and vibrant but might be harder for travel in remote areas.

Transportation

Major towns are connected by reliable road networks. 4x4 vehicles are recommended for safaris. Domestic flights are available to remote parks and lodges.

Language

English is the official language and widely spoken. Setswana is the national language and used in everyday conversations.
