Top Tourist Spots

Discover the best places to visit in Botswana for an unforgettable experience.

Gaborone

The capital city of Botswana, Gaborone offers a vibrant atmosphere with modern infrastructure, shopping malls, and cultural attractions like the National Museum and Gaborone Dam.

Maun

Known as the "gateway to the Okavango Delta," Maun is also home to vibrant markets, cultural experiences, and fishing trips along the Okavango River.

Tsodilo Hills

A UNESCO World Heritage site, Tsodilo Hills is known for its ancient rock art and cultural significance. It’s a fascinating place to explore Botswana’s history.

Baines' Baobabs

Famous for its ancient baobab trees, Baines' Baobabs offers striking landscapes and an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of Botswana’s wilderness.

Gcwihaba Caves (Drotsky's Caves)

Explore the fascinating limestone formations and unique underground environment of Gcwihaba Caves, located in the heart of the Kalahari Desert.

Matsieng Footprints

Discover the ancient footprints in Matsieng, believed to be thousands of years old, and learn about their spiritual significance to the Nguni people.

Kubu Island

Located in the Makgadikgadi Salt Pans, Kubu Island is famous for its ancient baobab trees and unique landscapes, making it a stunning and photogenic destination.

The National Museum and Art Gallery

Located in Gaborone, the National Museum and Art Gallery is home to collections that showcase Botswana's history, cultural heritage, and contemporary art scene.

Lion Park Resort

Located near Gaborone, the Lion Park Resort offers recreational activities such as water parks, amusement rides, and picnic spots for a fun-filled day with family and friends.

The Three Dikgosi Monument

The Three Dikgosi Monument in Gaborone is dedicated to Botswana's first three chiefs, offering visitors a chance to learn about the nation's leadership and rich history.

Okavango Delta

Experience the unique culture of the riverine communities in the Okavango Delta. Visit traditional villages to learn about the Bayei and Hambukushu people's way of life, their fishing techniques, and mokoro (dugout canoe) craftsmanship.

Sedudu Island Viewpoint

The scenic viewpoint overlooking Sedudu Island offers breathtaking views of the Chobe River and the island's wildlife. Learn about the historical territorial dispute and how it was peacefully resolved through international law.

Makgadikgadi Salt Pans

Experience the surreal beauty of camping on the world's largest salt pans. The otherworldly landscape offers incredible stargazing opportunities and the chance to witness the annual zebra migration during the green season.

Kalahari San Bushmen Villages

Visit a traditional San Bushmen village to learn ancient survival skills, hunting techniques, and the fascinating click language of Africa's oldest inhabitants. Experience their intimate connection with the Kalahari ecosystem.

Tsodilo Hills

Explore the "Louvre of the Desert" with over 4,500 ancient rock paintings dating back thousands of years. This UNESCO World Heritage Site holds spiritual significance for the San people and features stunning desert landscapes.

Tuli Block

Witness some of Africa's largest elephants against the dramatic sandstone formations of the Tuli Block. This area is also known for its ancient baobab trees and excellent birdwatching opportunities.

Nata Bird Sanctuary

Visit this community-run sanctuary to see spectacular flocks of flamingos and other waterbirds that gather at the Sua Pan during the rainy season. The sanctuary offers excellent birdwatching hides and walking trails.

Khama Rhino Sanctuary

This community-based wildlife project provides a safe haven for both black and white rhinos. Take a guided game drive to see these magnificent creatures along with other wildlife in a beautiful Kalahari sandveld environment.

Matsieng Footprints

Explore these mysterious rock engravings said to be the footprints of the first man and his animals emerging from the underworld. The site holds great cultural significance in Botswana's creation mythology.
