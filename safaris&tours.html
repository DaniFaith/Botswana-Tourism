Safaris & Tours

Discover unforgettable safari experiences and explore Botswana's breathtaking wilderness.

Game Drives

Explore Botswana's national parks and game reserves in open 4x4 vehicles with experienced guides. These drives offer the best chance to spot the "Big Five" (elephants, lions, leopards, rhinos, and buffalo) as well as a wide variety of other wildlife.

Best Locations:

Okavango Delta, Chobe National Park, Makgadikgadi Pans.

Walking Safari

A more intimate and personal safari experience, walking safaris allow tourists to get closer to nature by exploring the wilderness on foot with a trained guide. It’s a great way to learn about Botswana's flora and fauna up close.

Best Locations:

Okavango Delta, Moremi Game Reserve, Kalahari Desert.

Boat Safari

Explore the Okavango Delta or Chobe River by boat to see aquatic life, including hippos, crocodiles, and a variety of bird species. Boat safaris also offer excellent opportunities for photography.

Best Locations:

Okavango Delta, Chobe River, Linyanti Marshes.

Mokoro (Canoe) Safari

A traditional mokoro (dug-out canoe) safari allows tourists to glide silently through the shallow channels of the Okavango Delta, providing a unique perspective of the environment and a chance to spot wildlife along the banks.

Best Locations:

Okavango Delta.

Night Safari

For a different safari experience, night safaris let tourists discover nocturnal wildlife that is more active after dark, such as hyenas, owls, and leopards. Night drives often offer a chance to see the African wilderness from a new angle.

Best Locations:

Chobe National Park, Okavango Delta, Moremi Game Reserve.

Helicopter Safari

For those wanting to get an aerial view of Botswana’s iconic landscapes and wildlife, helicopter safaris provide an unmatched experience. The flight offers sweeping views of the Okavango Delta, Makgadikgadi Pans, and Chobe River.

Best Locations:

Okavango Delta, Chobe National Park.

Cultural Tours

Immerse yourself in the local culture by visiting traditional villages and communities. Learn about the culture, customs, and history of Botswana's indigenous people such as the San Bushmen, who have lived in the region for thousands of years.

Best Locations:

Maun, Ghanzi, Okavango Delta.

Hot Air Balloon Safari

For a bird’s-eye view of the African savannah, tourists can take a hot air balloon safari, offering panoramic views of wildlife and landscapes like never before. These tours typically include a champagne breakfast after landing.

Best Locations:

Okavango Delta, Chobe National Park.

Fishing Safaris

For those who enjoy fishing, Botswana offers some world-class fishing opportunities, especially for species like tigerfish and bream. Fishing safaris often take place on the Chobe River or the Okavango Delta.

Best Locations:

Chobe River, Okavango Delta.

Horseback Safaris

Horseback safaris allow tourists to get closer to the animals, as horses are less likely to disturb wildlife. This is a unique way to experience the African bush and gain a different perspective on the landscape.

Best Locations:

Okavango Delta, Makgadikgadi Pans.

Overnight Camping Safaris

For a more adventurous safari experience, overnight camping trips allow tourists to spend a night under the stars in the wild, experiencing the African bush at night. This can be combined with other safari activities like game drives or walking safaris.

Best Locations:

Okavango Delta, Moremi Game Reserve, Chobe National Park.

Bird Watching Safaris

Botswana is a haven for bird watchers, with over 500 bird species to be seen. Bird watching safaris offer an opportunity to see some of the most beautiful and rare birds in the world.

Best Locations:

Okavango Delta, Chobe National Park, Makgadikgadi Pans.
